Sources

Compilation of all notes, research, and interviews for insight

Sources used to create my list of steps:

“Planning Your Start-Up: A Checklist” by the Valley Small Business Development Center

⤷

    The ultimate checklist of steps for starting a strong, profitable business!
    1. Determine the feasibility of your business idea
    2. Prepare a business plan with loan proposal if required
    3. Assemble a management team
    4. Research financing options
    5. Research locations
    6. Choose a business Name
    7. Choose your legal structure
    8. Obtain a FEIN
    9. Determine state tax requirements (register with department of taxation)
    10. Register with the Virginia Employment Commission
    11. File Certificate of Fictitious name
    12. Apply for local busienss licenses
    13. Obtain insurance needed
    14. Consider professional or occupational licenses
    15. Open a business checking account
    16. Other considerations (employee info)

“Starting a New Business in Augusta County” by Augusta County Commissioner of Revenue Office

⤷

    1. Determine the business structure
    2. Register trade name
    3. Submit the business license application
    4. Obtain a zoning certificate or home occupation permit
    5. For contractors, obtain workers compensation info
    6. Submit the business license application with documents
    7. Register for business tangible personal property tax
    8. Register with state-level agencies

“Ten Steps to Start Your Business” by the U.S. Small Business Administration

⤷

    Steps:
    1. Conduct market research
    2. Write business plan
    3. Find funding for business
    4. Pick location
    5. Decide business type and structure
    6. Choose business name
    7. Register business
    8. Get federal and state tax IDs
    9. Apply for licenses and permits
    10. Open a business bank account

“How to Start a Business: A Small Business Checklist” by WGU

⤷

    Steps:
    1. Begin with a solid business idea
    2. Market research / identify target market
    3. Develop a business plan
    4. Get a small business loan
    5. Hire employees
    6. Improvise and adapt
    7. Be patient

“Launch Your Business” by the U.S. Small Business Administration

⤷

    Steps:
    1. Pick business location
    2. Choose business structure
    3. Choose business name
    4. Register business as legal entity
    5. Get federal and state tax IDs
    6. Apply for licenses and permits
    7. Open business bank account
    8. Get business insurance

“Start a New Business” by the State Corporation Commission

⤷

    Steps:
    1. Select business type
    2. Choose business name
    3. Select registered agent
    4. Register with SCC

Sources for Graphic Design Research:

“How to Start a Graphic Design Business: 16 Do's and Don'ts from the Pros” by Rasmussen University

⤷

    Summary of Do's and Don'ts:
    1. Your work has value
    2. Don't underestimate strong portfolio power
    3. Craft business plan
    4. Don't expect instant success
    5. Find a mentor
    6. Don't do work just for exposure
    7. Be authentic
    8. Don't lost sight of your 'why'
    9. Trust your instincts
    10. Don't say yes to every project
    11. Find your specialty or niche
    12. Diversify clients
    13. Familiarize yourself with copywrights
    14. Don't do work without contract
    15. Do good/donate time for what you are passionate about
    16. Don't quit your day job

“The Demand for Graphic Designers is Growing in 2023” by UK Blue Sky Graphics

⤷

    1. High demand for graphic designers
    2. “indispensable in the marketing world, where effective and creative marketing campaigns are critical to success”
    3. Growing digital age
    4. Help businesses stand out with stunning graphics
    5. Importance of branding and presentation
    6. Be passionate, have strong understanding of editing software, master art of layout and composition, have strong web design principles, keep up with current trends

Giacometti. Graphic Artists Guild Handbook: Pricing & Ethical Guidelines. 10th ed., Graphic Artists Guild, 2001.
