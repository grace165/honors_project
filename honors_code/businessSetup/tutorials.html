Writing a Business Plan

In this video, I walk through what is needed to write a succesful, clear Business plan that outlines the small business's procedures for the future.

Transcription

Hello! My name is Grace, and this is my tutorial on how to write a successful business plan. After this tutorial, you will be able to write a clear, readable business plan that outlines what your plan is for your small business's future. I'll start by walking through what you need in order to setup your business plan, and then we'll jump into the specifics of what needs to be clearly addressed in your plan. Let's get started!

Now, before writing your business plan, there are 10 things that you need to consider. These questions will help guide your thoughts and understanding of what is necessary when thinking of the long-term outlook for your business. This list can be found at the valley small business development center website, and I have attached the link to this slide and will put it in the description down below. So, to start, you have to begin thinking about what your product will look like. What product are you providing? What is the need? Is it solving a problem? Who is your market? And why will people want to buy it? And these questions are just here to get you thinking about what you will do with your product and what profit will be needed to have a sustainable business. So I'd consider going through these once or twice and just getting the details out before starting your actual business plan.

Next, we will discuss some more basic needs for your business plan. First, I am sure that you have thought about what you will name your business, but have you checked with the State Corporation Commission? This site will show you the availability for business names, and remember that there are certain naming conventions and rules also listed on this site. For example, all Limited Liability Companies need to have LLC somewhere in their name. And then you can also register for a fictitious name from there if you want to. The second thing you need to have ready for your business plan is your market research and who is going to be your target market. This will lead you to your initial setup costs and your considerations and options for financing. Also, make sure to have contacts ready for who your team is going to be when running your business. Who is managing bookkeeping? Are you hiring an attorney? Et cetera. Finally, think about the location of your business–either a physical storefront or online. You will need to file for a zoning application no matter where you're going to be working from, even if you are working from home and have an online business.

With those initial startup necessities filled out, you can also access other resources that will help you fill in your business plan. The Valley SBDC has a growth wheel model that I follow for the rest of this tutorial as well as financial worksheets and excel spreadsheets that are fill in the blank for your business costs and beginning investments. They also have useful checklists for what you need in your beginning stages of starting your small business.

Finally! Let's fill out our business plan! Gather all your resources we've been collecting and focus on being descriptive and thorough. Remember, a good business plan is key to starting a successful small business. Let's get into it!

So, here I am going to be using an example of a business plan outline that is available at the Valley SBDC website that I had mentioned earlier and I'll link in the description. So, on this front page here, for the business plan, you are going to fill in simple general personal information. Add who the owner of the business is, the address for the business, the date, and general contact information.

On the second page, focus on the details for these specific sections. The executive summary is where you are going to put your goals and mission statements for your business. This is actually going to be written last. So after you go through the business plan, you'll come back up here. And it's kind of like a summary of everything else you've written in these next couple pages. Then focus on the business concept. That's where you're going to describe your product info and customer info. Again linking to those target markets and market research that we've been collecting for your business plan. You'll think about the organization for your business, that includes the ownership and also information for having employees, if you are going to have any.

Moving on to the third page, you'll go over again more details with the organization here–talking about potential partnerships, allies, and suppliers–who you are going to be working with in the community and get your resources from. Then we are going to start talking about your business processes and that's how you'll go about your daily tasks. And then move on to customer relations–who are you networking with? How is your marketing going to work? Etc.

Finally, on the fourth page, you'll finish discussing your marketing with details about your branding and communications with PR...Talk about what social media accounts you are going to have, if you're going to use radio, television, etc. And then move on to operations where you'll discuss the details with financials like we discussed earlier with if you're going to get any loans, discuss the funding in general, production, deliveries, how are you going to make that work, IT systemsâ€¦and that sums up the fourth page for the business plan!

Now, this fifth page is optional. It's listed as the appendix where you can attach pages that will be useful after you've just gone through and listed the details for your business's functions, product information, and processes. I would recommend if you've got a physical storefront location already picked out that you list the blueprints for floorplans. On here it lists menus–perhaps if you're starting a small restaurant. I know that when I went through this process I made sure to add a startup cost worksheet and that can also be found in an Excel spreadsheet given to you by the Valley SBDC if you go to that resources tab that I discussed earlier.

In conclusion, I just want to say it one more time– “A good business plan sets the tone for the rest of the startup process.” If you are able to write a good business plan, that will be a strong foundation for the rest of your startup process. At the beginning of setting up a small business it can be a bit daunting, but if you have a strong foundation from the get–go, you have a strong plan, you know where your business is going to go in the future, you have the necessary resources to make that happen–itâ€™s going to be a much easier process for you. So, hopefully, by the end of this video, you've been able to follow through with the tutorial and gained some insight and necessary assets that you need in order to write your own business plan. I would suggest going to your local SBDC or check the Valley SBDC link that I've put in this video and in the description. And from there you'll be able to fill out that model for the business plan or you can write your own. Thank you!

In this video, I walk through and discuss the tedious process of registering with the SCC.

Transcription

Hello! My name is Grace, and welcome back to another one of my small business setup tutorials! Today, we will be discussing the official beginnings of your business–filing with the State Corporation Commission! This video will help guide you through the steps needed to set up your information and file for your articles of organization. Let's get started!

In today's video, we will cover how to create a login, checking availability of business names, and upload or file for the articles of organization. This example is also done through the SCC site for Virginia and is done for an LLC creation. I have also linked the SCC site to this page here, and I will put it in the description below.

First, we will discuss the part I had the most trouble with–setting up your SCC account!

To start, load up the SCC site and find the section asking you to sign in. It is the column located all the way to the left and asks for a username and password. Since we are just starting out, we are only going to hit the Green “Register” button. This will take you to a page asking you to fill out all of your personal information! You can read through the terms and conditions, and then choose a username and password and write them down somewhere safe! You will also want to make sure you are using your personal address, not the address for the business. You want to keep your account information in case you need to change your address in the future. After filling in your personal info, you will hit the “submit” button at the bottom of the page and it should send a code to your phone. Now, this is where I had issues. I could not get the code sent to my phone for some reason and had to email the SCC to receive a temporary code. Once I had received the temporary code, I also had issues because they could not validate my identity with their system. Instead, I had to fill out an Account Help Request Form, which we will go over next.

If you need to go to this step, this page will again ask for your personal information in creating your account. It says that they will only accept certain bills that provide your name and address, but I submitted a picture of the front of my driver's license and got my account created that way. After uploading your image or pdf file and submitting, it will take a day or 2 for them to create your account for you, but you will be set from there! (you will receive an email saying your account was setup given the username and password you provided in this form). If you were able to take the regular route and submit that way, you are also ready to use your account information and sign in, which is our next step!

Next, we move past the creation of your account to after you have signed in and are ready to navigate the dashboard.

Once signed in, you will open up to your Dashboard view. Here, you can see there are many things that you can access and do through your SCC account. Don't get too overwhelmed–we are only looking for the “Online Services” tab at the top.

Here, on the “Online Services” page, we are going to click on the “New Businesses” link under “Business Entities” at the top. This is how you are going to file for your articles of organization.

OK, we have successfully navigated to the creation of our new articles of organization, let's get started! This is what will take the most time to complete. It is best to get it done all at once, but there are options to access the filings again if you need to.

After clicking on the New Businesses link, we will be taken to the creating a business page. Here we will file for the Virginia Entity and select our business type. For this example, we will be choosing the Limited Liability Company or LLC. When choosing your business type, remember to do your research to find which type is best for you. Once you select the LLC type, another box comes up asking for the filing typeâ€”the options are Articles of Organization or Articles of Organization for a Professional LLC. Only choose the professional one if you are a recognized professional with certain certificationsâ€¦then hit continue at the bottom of the form to move to the next Step!

Now, it's finally time to check the availability of your business name! By now you should have checked your name on the SCC site following the steps on my website and can enter it now and check for the availability of that nameâ€¦Note, that Certain words and abbreviations are necessary for business types (for example, limited liability companies need to have an “LLC” abbreviation in their registration). You can check at this link listed on the slide, and I will put it in the description below, for the necessary pieces needed for your business name. Once you've chosen a name, hit next, and the system will let you continue if your name is available and fits the necessary requirements. Another note is that you can reserve a name if you cannot file all at once and are worried about saving the name, but it costs $10 and the SCC does not recommend doing this. It's easier to file all at once.

For the next step, you'll need to enter your information again and choose the duration of the business. In general, choose perpetual (forever) and you can close at any time. You also need an industry code if you apply to any of those–for a general business type, like ours, leave it as general. If you are an insurance or mortgage company or a religious group, then you will need to choose an industry code that applies to you. Once you have double checked every box, hit next at the bottom of the page to continue.

If you are a registered agent or have someone else acting as yours, then here is the step for you to organize that! If you need help, I have included the link for more information on this slide and in the description. As manager and owner of the LLC, I can be my own registered agent. Hit the “Create Individual RA” button. You'll fill in my personal information again and it will verify the address! You will want to pick the RA capacity that works for your titleâ€”for me, I picked the member or manager of the LLC, but there are other options that are more specific. Hit the checkbox indicating the individual is a resident of VA, and continue with the next button.

For the next step, you will need to list the information for the address of your principal office. Where will your business be doing business? For me, I have a graphic design business based out of my home, so I'll use my personal home address. If you have an online business working out of your home, you can do the same. Else, use your physical storefront or main office address. Hit next when you are finished!

This next step is simple. It is not required and will only be listed in the articles of organization for your business. You can choose to add a structure for your business and choose of the only 2 optionsâ€”manager managed or member managed. I chose to leave this blank. Once you are done with this decision, hit next.

Next, you have the opportunity if you choose to upload a document that will be acting as your articles of organization. This upload requires a review time period and will delay your approval. If you choose to do so, click the checkbox and hit the green “Attach” button to add your PDF uploads (they only accept PDFs with a maximum size of 50MB, but you can upload multiple attachments if you need to). I also skipped this step. When you are ready, hit the next button to move on!

Finally, we reach the signatures. Here, you simply type the name of the organizer (in this case your name if you are filing as an individual) and fill in your first and last name as well. Then hit the “Add”. You can edit these or delete them as well. And when you are done, hit next.

We are almost there! Now, you have the chance to review all of the information that you have added thus far. You can edit anything you need, but take the time to double check everything is correct and that you left nothing blank. Woo! You did it! The process of filing with the SCC can seem scary and tedious, but this is the exciting official start of your new small business! It is best to sit down and get this done all at once to ensure there are no errors in the system, but it should not take any longer than 30 minutes while reading all text boxes for ensuring correct inputs and information. Hopefully, after following this tutorial, you will feel confident about filing and get your new business officially started! Thank you for watching!

How to Obtain Your Federal EMployer ID Number

In this video, I walk through and discuss the process of filing with the IRS to obtain an FEIN for your small business.

Transcription

Hello! My name is Grace, and this is yet another tutorial in my series for starting a small business. Today, we will be talking about how to obtain your FEIN or Federal Employer Identification Number. Now, you might be asking, why do we need an EIN? The EIN acts as your business's Social Security Number when filing for taxes. Your business is like an individual and needs to be identified by the government for things like taxes and when dealing with employees. You also must have an FEIN for applying for a business license. So, now that you know the purpose behind an FEIN, how can a small business go about getting one? Let's get started!

To start, go to the IRS site and look under information for new businesses. I will add the link in the description below, but if you do a Google search for “IRS.gov EIN Assistant”, it will also pop up. First, we need to follow the steps listed on the page. Step 1 simply confirms that your business is located in the U.S. and that the person applying has a valid SSN available to use (that is the Social Security Number). The second step reminds the user that the application must be done in one session. Step 3 simply includes hitting the “Apply Online Now” button to begin your application!

Once on this main page, you will want to read through the information given before beginning the application. One thing to note is that you must complete this application in one sitting–one session like mentioned earlier, as that is less than 15 minutes. This application is very short and should not take that long. You should also note that your EIN is available immediately after applying. Once you've read and understood the information presented, which was also on the previous page, we can begin the application!

To start the application, the EIN assistant asks what your business type is. From our previous examples, we followed the Limited Liability Company, so we will do that again here. You should have already filed with the SCC before trying to get your EIN, so you should know which legal structure to follow for this step.

Next, the IRS site gives some information regarding the LLC business type to ensure that the reader understands what it is that they are applying for. There are simple definitions and information that we should already be aware of at this point to be applying for the FEIN, but the IRS is just making sure and gives multiple changes to change your structure before you get too far into this process. Once you feel comfortable knowing that you have chosen the right legal structure, you can hit continue.

Now, the site is asking for some basic information regarding the LLC. Fill in the box for how many members are in the LLC. For our example, I am the only person, so I put a 1. Then you pick the state in which the business is located. Again, after hitting the “Continue” button, the IRS site gives you more information regarding your choice to use the LLC legal structure and provides yet another chance to return to choose a different one. If you have read the information and feel confident in your LLC decision, you can hit continue and can move on.

Next, the IRS wants to know why we need an EIN. For us, we are starting a new business and need it for the operations of a small business. After picking the top button, hit continue. Here, we need to fill out the information for who is responsible in the LLC. For our example, as the only member of my LLC, I will put my own information in, including my SSN, and say that I am one of the owners, members, or the managing member of this LLC. Make sure to double check this information before moving forward again–we don't want any misspellings here!

Moving on, we need to fill out the information for the physical location of the business. For our example, I have an online business based out of my home. I will put my home address and home phone number. Make sure to check if the address is different from where you want your mail to be sent to. Since it's my home address, this works fine. Again, double check this information for any errors before moving forward.

Next, the EIN Assistant wants to know more about the LLC! You should have your legal name for the LLC from filing with the SCC. If you decided to have a fictitious trade name, fill that in as well. Fill in the information regarding the business's location and the date that you filed with the SCC will be used as the LLC start date. Double check the information, and then hit continue!

We are almost there! The IRS wants to know even more about your business and, particularly, what kind of business activities you will be performing. For our example, I answered no to all of the questionsâ€”I do not have a huge commercial vehicle in use for my business, nor is my business involving any gambling. I also do not need Form 720 for tax returns because my products and services do not have excise taxes placed on them (these would include the sale of alcohol, cigarettes, gasoline etc). This answer coincides with the next question, as well, and then I am the only one in my LLC so I answer no to the last question regarding employees too.

What's the next part about? You guessed it! The IRS wants to know even more specifics about your business and what you do! Pick from the list if applicable, but my example does not fit. For graphic design services, I picked other and was prompted again to describe my services provided.

FINALLY! We made it! Our application went through and now we make the choice of where we want our EIN confirmation letter sent. I chose to receive it online so that I could have it immediately. It comes as a PDF and is easy to read. Once you've chosen, hit continue for the final time to receive your Federal Employer Identification Number!

Congratulations! You now have your FEIN! You can use this to identify your business for tax purposes and file for your business license. The process for filing for the EIN is not hard, and hopefully, this tutorial made it even easier. Thank you for watching.
