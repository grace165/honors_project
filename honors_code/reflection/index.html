Reflection

This section covers my reflection on my honors project. I am using this essay to connect my experiences with this project to my overall college experience. I would also like to address everyone that has helped me with this project and list specifics below.

When I first started college, I had no idea where I was going in life. I knew that I was interested in working with computers, so I came to Bridgewater as a declared Computer Science major. After my first semester, I decided that I needed a challenge–I was so excited to add Business Administration as my second major and knew that it would be a good fit for me. I soon learned that double majoring required quite a bit of work. As my passions for computer science and business grew throughout my four years at Bridgewater, I knew that this final project needed to be a culmination of all the positive experiences I have had thus far. As I am entering my final semester in college, I know that all this hard work was well worth it; moreover, this challenging honors project is the perfect capstone for my college career. I was able to combine my interests in computer science, business, and graphic design to showcase my growth throughout my time at Bridgewater College. Hopefully, my personal improvements and ambition are evidently reflected through the effort I have put into this project.

In the end, I learned a lot from this project. Firstly, setting up a small business takes a lot more time and effort than I thought. When I began this project in April 2022, I thought I could do some research over the summer and finish the project before school started in the fall. I believed that I could do it all online without ever needing to go anywhere. I soon realized that this was not the case–the best resources I had during this project were the professionals I met with. Without them, this project would not have gone as smoothly. I learned how important it is to meet with experts in your community and how much knowledge they have to share. I also learned that, while there are many resources on the Internet, the best place to start when opening a new business is within your community. The Small Business Development Center (SBDC) at James Madison University was an invaluable asset for my project. The Government Center in Verona was also helpful in furthering my research and understanding. Furthermore, the professors at Bridgewater College provided needed expertise. Overall, I learned a lot about how a small business is dependent upon the support and knowledge of the professionals in the community.

If I could go back to the beginning of this project and start over, I would only change one thing: I would have started earlier. The hardest part of this project was the timing. I had 18 credits this fall, and the workload was heavier than anticipated. I spent almost every day of this academic semester working on my honors project. I also had planned to complete more tasks associated with this honors project–I wanted to meet with more experienced experts in the industry but ran out of time. It took much longer than I thought to be able to file for my Articles of Organization and apply for a zoning permit and business license. My progress was halted multiple times by the slow bureaucracy of the Government Center. The setbacks further slowed the advancement of my website, which I already had trouble creating using Microsoft Azure. I did not expect to hit so many roadblocks because I thought that I was prepared and had ample enough time to cover any issues. Still, I was able to accomplish a lot, and I am happy with the outcome of my honors project.

Reflecting on this project, I am truly proud of the progress I made in one semester. While I was unable to accomplish everything I had planned for, I was able to grow and master the steps I covered while setting up my own small business. I feel that my interests in computer science, business, and graphics design were well represented as the inspiration behind this project and will continue to push my daily endeavors. I am truly grateful for the assistance from experts in the industry that have guided me on the successful start of my graphic design business. While I am unsure of the future of my small business, I am so excited to continue to learn and grow my understanding of what it means to be an entrepreneur.

Special thanks to my advising professor, Dr. Daniel Finseth, and the other members of my Honors Project committee, Dr. Eric McGregor and Ron Alabanza. Thank you to Allison Dugan, Greg Godsey, Derek Smiley, and Rebekah Castle for taking the time to meet with me and show interest in my project.
