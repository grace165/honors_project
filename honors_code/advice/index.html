Advice

This section is used to keep track of pieces of information I found helpful in creating this project. These bits of advice are combinations of my thoughts and strategies when going through the startup process.

General Startup

TALK TO PROFESSIONALS! Use your resources–especially free ones! When I began this project, I assumed I could do all of this alone. I would not have been succesful had I not met with my local SBDC and government center. The valuable resources and aid I recieved from all of the professionals I worked with could not have been found on my own.

Networking

Join your local chamber of commerce! Active involvement in this organization and others will lead to more connections and further attention for your business. The more active you are in your community, the more people you will meet–valuable connections help your business grow!

Client Relations

This is one of the most important parts of your business plan. Specifically, I suggest focusing a large portion of your business plan on how you will achieve positive relations with clients. My advice is to focus on only a few clients at a time to ensure the quality of your work matches what the customer needs.

Marketing

I stress the importance of having a visually inviting website. Use social media and business cards as tools to get customers onto your site. Focus on the design of each element and how this will encourage customers to return in the future.

Contracts

Hire an attorney! The last thing you want is legal issues. An attorney can write contracts and ensure your business's safety against damaging lawsuits.
